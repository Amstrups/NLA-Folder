    \section{Relatable values}

From using our SVD-operation on our \(B\)-matrix, we get \(U\), \(S\)
and \(V^T\). For our \(u\), it resembles our origional rotation matrix,
with a twist. Our rotation matrix is given by:
\[ R = \begin{bmatrix} c && -s \\ s && c \end{bmatrix},\] while our
\(U\) is roughly given by:
\[ U \approx \begin{bmatrix} -c && -s \\ -s && c \end{bmatrix}.\] Our
\(S\) is a form of numerical representation for the shape of our B. Each
entrance in the vector \(S\) does not seem to relate to anything other
than the other dimensions in \(S\). Our \(S_0 ~= 3\cdot S_1\), which
makes sense, since our \(x = 3cos(t)\).
